Wonderbag

Wonderbag is an NGO, addressing the open fire cooking problem that releases large amounts of CO2, and jeopardizes the health of women and children. Wonderbag is a simple but revolutionary non-electric, portable slow cooker, that uses clever insulation to allow food that has been brought to a boil to continue slow cooking or warm while in the bag.

I have been working with wonderbag to quantify, model, and visualize the CO2 emission reductions from the mass adoption of their product. Currently, we are in the model review process and intend to publish it as a dashboard on the wonderbag website soon after.

blitz

As a part of my capstone project, my teammates and I built an autonomous electric vehicle charging solution. The picture above showcases our intended final product, while the video below shows a working prototype.

As the use of electric vehicles and development of autonomous vehicle increases, a need exists to identify a solution to autonomously charge EVs in an efficient and cost-effective manner.

Our solution is to create a robotic arm that can be retrofitted to existing charging stations in order to automate the charging process. By using a vision system and an object detection algorithm, our product is able to identify the vehicle charge port location and direct the charging handle into the port.

Team Members: Sanan Akhundzada, Michael Chau-Nguyen, Huzaifa Khan, Kevin Qiu, Baber Siddiqui, Mazen Zohiry

Hult Prize 2020 - Plant It

Hult Prize is an annual competition, that challenges college students to solve pressing social issues around topics such as food security, water access, energy, and education. Each year the Hult team sets a problem theme, and in 2020 it was Climate Change.

In a group of four, our team developed and pitched a community vertical farming social enterprise. Our plan consisted of taking advantage of the empty wasted space on condominium rooftops to build vertical farms for the tenants. We pitched this idea to vertical farming companies and local developers and received positive feedback from both sides.

Team Members: Siya Agarwal, Ankita Mishra, Falah Shazib
